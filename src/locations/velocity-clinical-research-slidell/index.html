- name: Cherie Oertling, MD role: Principal Investigator image: bio_html: >-

Dr. Cherie Oertling earned her undergraduate degree at Louisiana State University before obtaining her medical degree at Louisiana State University School of Medicine in New Orleans. Upon graduation she remained in New Orleans to complete her residency in pediatrics at Children's Hospital and the Medical Center of New Orleans.

In 1996 Dr. Oertling began practicing in Slidell, LA where she has served children and their families for over 25 years. Dr. Oertling manages her practice along with her husband and fellow pediatrician, Dr. Robert Oertling.

Dr. Oertling entered the clinical trials world in 2022. Her interest in clinical trials began with the advent of the COVID pandemic. She hopes her participation in trials will bring new medications, vaccinations, and treatments to the children in her community and across the world.

- name: Mary Hess, FNP role: Sub Investigator image: bio_html: >-

Mary Hess joined the field of clinical research in 2022 as a full-time sub investigator for Velocity Clinical Research, Slidell. She initially became interested in research in the late 90’s while working for an endocrinologist from 1999-2007. Aside from research, Mary is no novice to the medical field with over 30 years of experience as a nurse.

Mary earned undergraduate nursing degrees from Elizabethtown Community College and Bellarmine University. Soon afterward, she received her Master of Science in Nursing from Loma Linda University. She then got her Master of Family Nurse Practitioner from Emory. Finally, she obtained her Doctor of Health Administration from University of Phoenix.

In the public sector, Mary also served in the U.S. Army Reserves for five years, U.S. Army Nurse Corps Active Duty for five years, and the U.S. Air Force Reserves for three years. In the private sector, Mary has held numerous nursing roles across Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, Georgia, Virginia, and Montana.

Our Story

Conducting Clinical Trials Since 2019

Velocity Clinical Research, Slidell was founded in 2019 as a part of MedPharmics. Since its inception, the site has previously conducted trials in Infectious Diseases and Women’s Health. Slidell also now conducts pediatric trials and will continue to expand to new therapeutic areas.

The site is easily accessible as it near I-12 on Brownswitch Road, right after the roundabout. The stand-alone building is fully equipped to accommodate various types of clinical trials.

The Slidell team is committed to being a resource for study volunteers, to providing the highest quality of patient care with compassion and kindness and to advancing medicine though research. By joining Velocity Clinical Research organization in August 2022, they added new efficiencies and integration to their already comprehensive approaches to conducting clinical trials.

Our Experienced Research Team

The Velocity Slidell team of physician investigators and clinical research professionals are experienced clinical researchers. All physicians are board certified in their specialty area(s) and all research staff is trained in clinical research regulations, including GCP and HIPAA. Training records and certifications are maintained. Our clinical team is dedicated to conducting quality research in a professional and caring environment and strives on collecting quality data.
