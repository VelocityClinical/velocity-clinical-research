Gregg Lucksinger, M.D. received his medical degree from the University of Texas Health Science Center in San Antonio, TX and completed his residency in Family Medicine at Central Texas Medical Foundation / Brackenridge Hospital in Austin.  He is board certified in Family Medicine and has more than twenty years of clinical research experience as a Principal Investigator. The bulk of this research has consisted of Phase 2-3 studies of vaccines and antivirals or therapeutics for lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, migraine, chronic pain and cardiovascular and endocrine/metabolic diseases. After 15 years of practice in both inpatient and outpatient settings and 20 years with various clinical research organizations in the Austin area, Dr. Lucksinger is now the lead Principal Investigator at Velocity Medford.  In his free time, he enjoys gardening and cooking with his wife Sara, scuba diving, snow skiing and showing his artistic side as a glass blower.

Dr. Ryan B. Israelsen joined the Velocity Clinical Research in 2018. Dr. Israelsen received his bachelors of science in biological engineering from Utah State University. He attended medical school at the University of Utah then was trained in Pediatrics and Internal Medicine at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center. Dr. Israelsen is board certified in both pediatrics and internal medicine.

Dr. Israelsen completed fellowship training in allergy and immunology at the University of Colorado and Children’s Hospital of Colorado. In fellowship training Dr. Israelsen was involved in food allergy research, drug allergy, and was involved in numerous oral food challenges (testing for food allergies). Dr. Israelsen was also involved in the clinical care of children and adults with immunodeficiencies, autoimmune problems, and immune dysregulation.

Dr. Israelsen is a fellow of both the American Academy of Allergy, Asthma and Immunology and the American College of Allergy Asthma and Immunology. He has been involved in research regarding Crohn’s disease, immunodeficiency, immune dysregulation, Rituximab therapy for autoimmune diseases, and Penicillin allergy. He is the author on scientific papers for food allergy and asthma therapies and has been involved in food allergy, asthma and allergy research.

Outside of medical interests, Dr. Israelsen enjoys spending time with his wife, McKenzie, and 4 children. He also enjoys running, biking, hiking, rock climbing, good books, and chocolate chip cookies.

Velocity Clinical Research, Medford

Velocity Clinical Research, Medford, formerly Clinical Research Institute of Southern Oregon, is part of the Velocity Clinical Research organization, a wholly owned Site Organization, providing excellence in patient care, high quality data and fully integrated research sites.

Our Mission

Velocity’s mission is to bring innovative medical treatments to patients. We are committed to making clinical trials succeed by generating high quality data from as many patients as possible, as quickly as possible while providing exemplary patient care at every step.

Our Story

Conducting Clinical Trials Since 1997

Velocity Clinical Research, Medford was founded by Dr. Edwin Kerwin, a Board Certified Respiratory, Allergy and Clinical Immunology specialist, in 1997 as Clinical Research Institute of Southern Oregon. As an independent dedicated research facility, Velocity Medford has long been a foremost respiratory clinical research center, with a core focus on Allergy, Asthma and COPD clinical studies. Medford is often one of the top enrolling sites nationally for these respiratory indications and has also developed additional expertise in atopic dermatitis and vaccine studies. In addition, selected members of the research team process in-depth knowledge of spirometry assessments.

The site is conveniently located just 10 minutes from the Medford airport with easy access and free parking. Metropolitan Medford has a population of 216,000 with another 300,000 residents in the surrounding counties. A robust database of potential study participants supports other recruiting efforts in this geographic area. With more than 8,500 square-feet of dedicated research space, they can efficiently and effectively conduct multiple concurrent studies. All trials are performed in accordance with ICH and FDA guidelines, and in compliance with GCP.

The Velocity Medford team is committed to being a resource for study volunteers, to providing the highest quality of patient care with compassion and kindness and to advancing medicine though research. By becoming part of the Velocity Clinical Research organization in 2017, they added new efficiencies and integration to their already comprehensive approaches to conducting clinical trials.

Our Experienced Research Team

The Velocity Medford team of physician investigators and clinical research professionals are experienced clinical researchers. All physicians are board certified in their specialty area(s) and all research staff is trained in clinical research regulations, including GCP and HIPAA. Training records and certifications are maintained. Our clinical team is dedicated to conducting quality research in a professional and caring environment and strives on collecting quality data.
